Potential links between synesthesia  and other conditions

 

The conditions shown below may also be linked to synesthesia, but additional studies are required before we can say for certain whether there is a  definite link. 

Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD) Carmichael et al. (ref 1) found an early suggestion that synesthesia might be linked to OCD. This was a trend in the data, but could not be validated or replicated because the number of people with synesthesia is relatively small. So there is no clear evidence of this link, although a significant link to anxiety disorder â€“ itself linked to OCD. We therefore flag this non-significant trend for clinicians.

Irritable Bowel syndrome (IBS) IBS is a  visceral hypersensitivity in which patients also happen to show sensitivity to external stimuli (e.g., sound, ref 2). Carruthers, Miller, Tarrier, and Whorwell  (ref 29) screened a group of 200 patients with irritable bowel syndrome. Carruthers and colleagues found approximately three times the expected rate of synaesthesia in IBS patients compared with a non-clinical control sample. However a study by Carmichael et al. (ref 1) failed to find any link between synesthesia and IBS. 

Mutiple sclerosis (MS) In three independent synesthete cohorts across three countries (United States; France; and United Kingdom), Simner et al (ref 4) opportunistically found indicators suggestive of increased susceptability to MS (ref 5) in synesthetes when brain scans were performed. Each of the synesthetes identified presented with MRI features suggestive of MS but without overt clinical symptoms (known as radiologically isolated syndrome (RIS).
