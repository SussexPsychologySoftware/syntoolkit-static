Positive schizotypy and synaesthesia

 

Schizotypy is continuum of personality characteristics relating to unusual beliefs and experiences. At one end of the continuum are simple imaginative states, and on the other end are extreme states of mind related to psychosis or schizophrenia. Schizotypy does not necessarily imply illness -- some aspects of schizotypy can be beneficial. Two studies (refs 1,2).    Studies have shown that synaesthetes score higher in Positive Schizotypy (belief in the paranormal, strong sensory sensations, heightened imagery) a trait also linked to creativity and artistic achievement.
