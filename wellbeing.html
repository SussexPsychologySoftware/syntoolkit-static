Synaesthesia and well-being

Experiencing pain from synaesthesia: Unfortunately, there are several forms of synaesthesia that can cause pain. One example is called mirror-touch synaesthesia, in which children can experience pain when they see other children who have hurt themselves. In other types of synaesthesia, everyday pain (e.g., a headache) is accompanied by synaesthetic colours. For this type of synaesthesia, children sometimes report that thinking very hard about colours and synaesthesia can cause flashes of pain. Suggested support: You might treat the reports of pain the same way you would treat any other report of pain at school. Also understand that a child with mirror-touch is not being self-oriented just because he/she seeks attention when another child is hurt.

Anxiety: Adults and children with synaesthesia are more likely to suffer from anxiety than the general population. For example, in one study of children, synaesthetes. were more than twice as likely to suffer from anxiety than non-synaesthetes (ref 1). Suggested support: Treat the child like any other child in your class that suffers from anxiety, providing safe space and strategies to allow them to manage their feelings.

References

1. Simner, et al. (2021). Wellbeing differences in children with synaesthesia: anxiety and mood regulation. Frontiers in Bioscience (Elite Edition), 13.
