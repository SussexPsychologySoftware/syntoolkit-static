Educational and well-being issues: moderate to severe negative impacts 

In some cases, synaesthesia might have a severe impact on the child’s education and wellbeing. Some synaesthetic experiences are uncomfortable, or even painful. Synaesthesia can sometimes cause sickly patterns in front of the eyes, unpleasant tastes in the mouth, and even sensations of pain (eg. triggered by observing others in pain, in mirror-touch synaesthesia). These could result in avoidance behaviours, for instance avoiding letters, numbers or words that trigger their uncomfortable synaesthetic experience. This can impact on progress at school, excluding them from reading, maths, writing, and other tasks. We recommend working with school professionals and the child’s parents to better understand any adverse experiences, and to develop the most effective management strategy.  Although this app tests for only two forms of synaesthesia, these often co-occur with other variants (ref 1).  So validating one form can also suggest the presence of others. You can read a glossary of different types of synaesthesia here. The type of management strategy depends on the form and severity of the synaesthesia.
