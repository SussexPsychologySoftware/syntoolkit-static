What is grapheme- personification synaesthesia? Grapheme-personification synaesthesia is a lifelong feeling that letters and numbers have personalities or genders; for example, sensing that the letter A feels like an extraverted male, or that the number 7 is a generous female. Found in 1-2% of the population (ref 1) this form of synaesthesia has been traced to small differences in the corpus callosum of synaesthetes, a region of the brain known to be involved in the social self (ref 2). The personifications reported by letters and numbers are unchanging over time, and often complex. Common descriptions involve gender (e.g., the number is female), personality (e.g., the number is kind), physical appearance (e.g., the number is haggard-looking), occupation (e.g., the number is a businessman), cognitive attributes (e.g., the number is intelligent), familial relationships (e.g., the number is mother, friend to another number), and so on. Grapheme-personification synaesthesia has many different names; some names reflect the fact it can be triggered not just by graphemes (letters or numbers) but by any ordered sequence, including days-of-the-week, months-of-the-year, and so on. Hence it is sometimes called sequence-personality synaesthesia, or sequence-personification synaesthesia, or ordinal linguistic personification (this latter is especially common on the world wide web, where synaesthetes have posted many descriptions of their synesthesia, such as our pictured example). It can also be named more specifically, for example as letter-personification synaesthesia if triggered only by letters.
